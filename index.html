One program, not four plans

How the channels play together

By 2028, a single fan could get the same matchday alert by email, SMS, and push. Governance decided now prevents that — and makes each channel better at its own job.

Channel roles

Each message type has a home channel. Email carries story and depth. SMS owns urgency and deadlines. Push owns in-app moments and matchday utility. The website is where everything resolves.

When a message could go anywhere, the channel with the clearest job wins — and the others stay quiet.

Frequency care

A shared view of contact frequency across email, SMS, and push, with tighter limits on SMS than any other channel. Founding Members hear from us more — but always with more value, never just more volume.

Consent stewardship

Every audience knows what they signed up for. The Fan Fest cohort gets a proper introduction rather than a surprise. SMS consent is explicit and separate from email. Preference centers let fans tune, not just leave.

Shared dependencies

Ticketing and account integration touches every channel — deposit leads in email, ticket links on the web, account access in the app. Decisions are made once, in the app vendor process, and inherited everywhere.

Tentpole playbook

Every major moment — reveal, coach, signings, schedule, presale — runs the same coordinated sequence: SMS breaks it, push echoes it in-app, email tells the full story, and the website carries it forward.

Every fan has a path

Retention isn't only for deposit holders. Engaged fans who haven't converted move into re-engagement tracks — waitlists, single-match interest, community events — so the funnel never dead-ends.

The foundation underneath

One fan, one profile

Every touchpoint in this roadmap — a Fan Fest opt-in, a Digioh form fill, a Ticketmaster deposit, an app download — resolves to a single fan profile. Our data team manages this through Microsoft Dynamics CRM and the Hightouch CDP, stitching identity across systems and syncing enriched audiences back into our activation platforms. Marketing builds on that foundation; the data team owns it. That separation keeps this roadmap focused on fan experience while guaranteeing the segmentation and personalization it depends on.

Measurement philosophy

Aggressive by design.
Smarter every quarter.

This is the launch of a brand that has never existed. Rather than anchoring to targets invented before we have a single season of data, we move aggressively, watch closely, and feed what we learn directly into the next campaign. The first two years are the learning engine for everything after.

We watch

Database growth by source, engagement across channels, Founding Member conversion behavior, content resonance, and app adoption among our most committed fans.

We learn

Which acquisition sources produce fans who stay. Which stories move people to act. When urgency helps and when it burns trust. What Founding Members actually use.

We apply

Every insight feeds forward: welcome journeys tuned by source performance, conversion campaigns shaped by real engagement signals, 2028 season plans built on two years of observed behavior.

We formalize later

Hard benchmarks arrive when we have a baseline worth benchmarking against — after launch behavior, a full conversion cycle, and the first season give us real numbers to hold ourselves to.
